📊 Comparatives & Superlatives

Compare, discuss, and persuade — in English.

📐

How it works — in 30 seconds

Base adjective
fast
Comparative (+er / more)
faster · more comfortable
Superlative (the +est / most)
the fastest · the most comfortable
Short adjectives (1 syllable) → add -er / -est
Long adjectives (3+ syllables) → use more / the most
2-syllable adjectives ending -y → change to -ier / the -iest (e.g. busy → busier)

🏖️ Scenario 1 — Holiday Dilemma

Choosing between two very different holidays
A2 ▼

Situation: You and a friend are planning a holiday together. You have different ideas about what the perfect holiday looks like. Try to agree on one option.

👤 Person A — Beach Lover
  • You want a beach holiday in Spain.
  • You think it's much more relaxing than a city trip.
  • It's cheaper and the weather is better.
  • Ask your partner why they prefer the city.
👤 Person B — City Explorer
  • You want a city trip to Prague or Paris.
  • You think it's more interesting and educational.
  • There's more to do — museums, food, culture.
  • Try to persuade Person A it's the better choice.
🗣️ Useful language
"A beach holiday is far more relaxing because…" "But city trips are much more educational!" "Spain is a lot cheaper than Paris." "The best thing about … is…" "I think … is less stressful."

💼 Scenario 2 — Job Decision

Advising a friend on two job offers
A2+ ▼

Situation: Person B has two job offers and can't decide. Person A is their trusted friend and advisor. Help them compare the offers and decide.

👤 Person A — The Friend / Advisor
  • Ask about salary, hours, and location.
  • Give your opinion using comparatives.
  • Think about: which is more secure? Which is better for the future?
  • Help your friend make the best decision.
👤 Person B — Two Job Offers
  • Job 1: Marketing manager, high salary, very stressful, long hours, no remote work.
  • Job 2: Content creator, lower salary, flexible hours, fully remote, creative work.
  • Share the details and your feelings. Ask for advice.
🗣️ Useful language
"Job 1 is better paid, but much more stressful." "Job 2 is more flexible and less tiring." "In the long run, which is more secure?" "The most important thing for me is…" "I think … is a far better option because…"

🏠 Scenario 3 — Flat Hunting

Choosing between two apartments
A2+ ▼

Situation: You are roommates looking for a new flat. You both have different priorities. Try to agree on the best option.

👤 Person A — Priorities
  • Flat A: city centre, smaller, more expensive (€900/month), 5 min from work.
  • You want Flat A — it's more convenient and safer.
  • You think location is more important than size.
👤 Person B — Priorities
  • Flat B: suburbs, bigger, cheaper (€600/month), 40 min from work by bus.
  • You want Flat B — it's much bigger and quieter.
  • You think price and space are more important than location.
🗣️ Useful language
"Flat A is more convenient but less affordable." "Flat B is by far the better deal financially." "Which is more important — price or location?" "I'd say … is the smarter choice because…" "The biggest advantage of … is…"
